Angular Lifecycle Hooks

What is Lifecycle hooks in Angular

Lifecycle hooks in Angular are methods that are automatically called at specific points in a component's lifecycle. They provide a way to perform actions at different stages of a component's existence, such as initialization, updates, and destruction.

Common lifecycle hooks:
  • ngOnInit(): Called once after the component's constructor and initial data binding are complete.
  • ngOnChanges(): Called whenever a component's input properties change.
  • ngDoCheck(): Called after every change detection run, whether or not the component's bindings have changed.
  • ngAfterContentInit(): Called after Angular projects content into the component's view.
  • ngAfterContentChecked(): Called after every check of the component's content.
  • ngAfterViewInit(): Called after Angular has initialized the component's view and child views.
  • ngAfterViewChecked(): Called after every check of the component's view and child views.
  • ngOnDestroy(): Called when the component is about to be destroyed.

Use cases for lifecycle hooks:
  • Initialization: Perform tasks like fetching data or setting up subscriptions in ngOnInit.
  • Data updates: React to changes in input properties in ngOnChanges.
  • DOM manipulation: Perform DOM manipulations after the view is initialized in ngAfterViewInit.
  • Cleanup: Clean up resources like subscriptions or timers in ngOnDestroy.

By understanding and using lifecycle hooks effectively, you can create more robust and efficient Angular components.
